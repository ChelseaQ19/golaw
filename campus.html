These arenas are usually multi-purpose facilities that can host a wide range of events throughout the year, including basketball games, hockey matches, and concerts by famous musicians. The arena's seating arrangement is carefully planned to provide a comfortable experience for the spectators, with good visibility and acoustics. The arena also includes facilities for players, including locker rooms, showers, and training areas. The playing surface itself is also carefully designed to meet the specific requirements of the sport being played, with considerations such as lighting, flooring, and netting. Additionally, sports arenas are usually equipped with state-of-the-art technology, including scoreboards, video screens, and sound systems, which are essential for creating a memorable and exciting experience for fans. Overall, sports arenas are essential hubs of entertainment and a source of community pride, bringing people together to enjoy the excitement of sports and entertainment.

Luke Wadding Library on the Cork Road Campus is the Institute's main library and is one of the largest and most innovative third-level libraries in Ireland. Covering 6,000 sq. metres, with a range of resources and services and in excess of 1,000 reader spaces, the library is well-equipped to support the diverse learning needs of staff and students at the Institute. The building includes a dedicated learning centre, Special Collections room, staff reading area, and group study area. It is also home to the Academic Skills Centre, the eLearning Support Unit, and a Careers Advice centre.The library collection comprises over 180,000 print items, and over 120,000 eBook titles. In addition, the library houses over 340 hardcopy journals, and provides access to thousands of online journals through our database collections. There are over 150 student workstations available in the building with full Internet access and the library is a wireless zone. Luke Wadding Library is an energy efficient 'green' building and has won important architectural and Department of the Environment sustainable building awards. The building underpins Waterford Institute of Technology's commitment to being a regional centre of excellence and a world-class learning environment.
